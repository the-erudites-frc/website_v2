Read Before Submitting

Participating on an FRC team is a major time commitment, especially during build season (January - March) as we are only given eight weeks to design, build, and program our competition robot. As a member, your teammates will rely on you to do your part. By applying, you agree to this time commitment. Please note that schoolwork is not an excuse for poor commitment during the season. You will also be expected to attend in-person or online meetings in Stouffville. Additionally, there will be a fee of $150 per member. We kindly request this fee to cover our initial registration expenses. Rest assured, if we are successful with sponsorship funds, we will promptly reimburse you.This ensures that tournament Registration fees can be met and will allow the team to compete. If you cannot pay this fee, there are options available to help you, so upon acceptance to the team, you are encouraged to talk to the team's Co-Captains. This should not hold you back from applying! All team members are expected to volunteer at events our team will host/partner with for the community. It is embedded in our team values to spread our love for STEM with those around us. In our team, we really value honesty. If for any reason you can't get your work done in a week, just give us a heads-up. We understand that things can go off track sometimes, and it's totally fine to admit it. Just remember, honesty is key, so don't hesitate to let us know if things didn't go as planned. Feel free to contact TheEruditesfrc@gmail.com for financial support and other questions.
